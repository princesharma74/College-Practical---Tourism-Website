City of historical and cultural importance!

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city has a rich history, and has been a centre of learning and culture since the days of the Maurya and Gupta empires.

Today, Patna is a bustling metropolis, and is home to a number of educational institutions, as well as historical and cultural sites. The city is also a major economic hub, and is a great place to do business.

If you're looking for a city with a rich history and culture, and a bright future, then come to Patna!

Golghar

Golghar,Patna is a famous historical and tourist place in Patna, Bihar, India. It is a large granary constructed by Captain John Garstin of the British East India Company in 1786. The Golghar has a height of 29.1 m from the ground level and a staircase inside that encircles the structure providing access to the top. There is a porthole at the top from which one can have a bird's eye view of the entire city and the Ganges river. The Golghar was built to store grains in the event of a famine. The Golghar has been used for many purposes since its construction. It was used as a platform to watch the first total solar eclipse of 29th March 1868. It was also used as a lighthouse and a signals post. The Golghar has become a tourist attraction in recent years.
