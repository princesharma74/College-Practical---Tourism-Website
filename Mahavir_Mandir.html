City of historical and cultural importance!

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city has a rich history, and has been a centre of learning and culture since the days of the Maurya and Gupta empires.

Today, Patna is a bustling metropolis, and is home to a number of educational institutions, as well as historical and cultural sites. The city is also a major economic hub, and is a great place to do business.

Mahavir Mandir

Mahavir Mandir is a Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Hanuman, located in the city of Patna, Bihar, India. The temple is situated on the banks of the Ganges River, and is one of the most popular tourist destinations in the city. The temple was built in the year 1974 by the then Chief minister of Bihar, Late Shri Krishna Sinha. The temple is surrounded by a park, and has a pond in front of it. The main entrance of the temple is through a gate, which has a statue of Lord Hanuman on top of it. There are two other gates to the temple, one on the left side and one on the right side. The temple complex also has a library, a museum, and a guest house. The temple is open from early morning to evening, and the puja (worship) timings are from sunrise to sunset.

The Mahavir Mandir is one of the most important Hindu temples in Bihar, and is visited by thousands of pilgrims every year. The temple is considered to be very sacred, and is said to be the abode of Lord Hanuman.
