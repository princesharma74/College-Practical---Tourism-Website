City of historical and cultural importance!

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city has a rich history, and has been a centre of learning and culture since the days of the Maurya and Gupta empires.

Today, Patna is a bustling metropolis, and is home to a number of educational institutions, as well as historical and cultural sites. The city is also a major economic hub, and is a great place to do business.

If you're looking for a city with a rich history and culture, and a bright future, then come to Patna!

1. Chotaimambara

Chotaimambara is a beautiful and ancient temple in Patna, India. It is believed to have been built in the 11th century by King Chota Raja, and is dedicated to the Hindu god Vishnu. The temple is known for its intricate carvings and beautiful architecture, and is a popular tourist destination.

2. Kila Moti Masjid

Kila Moti Masjid is a mosque in Patna, India. It was built in 1620 by Mughal Emperor Jahangir. The mosque is located in the heart of the city, near the Gandhi Maidan. It is one of the largest mosques in Patna and can accommodate up to 5,000 people. The mosque has a large courtyard and a marble prayer hall. The mosque is also known for its beautiful architecture and is a popular tourist attraction.

3. Mahatma Gandhi Setu

The Mahatma Gandhi Setu, also known as the Gandhi Bridge, is a bridge over the river Ganges in Bihar, India. It connects the city of Patna in the south to Hajipur in the north. The bridge is one of the longest bridges in the world, with a length of 5,750 meters. It was built in the 1980s to replace the old bridge that was built in the 1930s. The Mahatma Gandhi Setu is named after the Indian independence leader Mahatma Gandhi.

4. Golghar

Golghar is a large dome-shaped structure located in Patna, Bihar, India. Built by Captain John Garstin in 1786, it has a circumference of 140 m and is 29.8 m high. It was built as a granary to store grain for the British Garstin had seen a similar structure in St. Petersburg and was inspired to build one in Patna. The Golghar has a unique architecture and was used as a model for several buildings in the region. It is a popular tourist spot and offers a panoramic view of the city.

5. Patna Museum

The Patna Museum is a repository of a rich and varied collection of antiquities, paintings, coins, sculptures, and natural history specimens. The museum has a rare collection of Archaeological finds dating back to the pre-historic, Mauryan, Sunga, Kushana, Gupta and Pala periods. The museum also houses a rich collection of coins, sculptures, paintings, and natural history specimens. The museum is a must-visit for history buffs and curious minds alike.

6. Agam Kuan

Agam Kuan is a well that is believed to date back to the time of the Mauryan Empire in Patna. The well is located in a park that also contains ruins of an ancient fort. The well is said to be where Ashoka's grandson, Kunala, was allegedly blinded. Today, the well is a popular spot for locals and tourists alike to learn about the history of Patna.

7. Patna Planetarium

The Patna Planetarium is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Patna. It is situated in the heart of the city and is easily accessible by public transport. The planetarium offers a wide range of educational and entertaining shows for all ages. It is a great place to learn about the night sky and the planets. The planetarium also has a museum which has a collection of rare and interesting astronomical objects.

8. Sanjay Gandhi Jaivik Udyan

Sanjay Gandhi Jaivik Udyan is a zoological park in Patna, the capital of the Indian state of Bihar. The park is named after Sanjay Gandhi, the son of former Prime Minister of India Indira Gandhi. The park was inaugurated by then Chief Minister of Bihar, Jagannath Mishra, on 10 February 1981. The park covers an area of 105 hectares and is home to a variety of flora and fauna. The park has a variety of animals including tigers, lions, leopards, bears, deer, and monkeys. The park also has a bird sanctuary which is home to a variety of birds such as peacocks, ducks, and parrots.

9. Mahavir Mandir

Mahavir Mandir is a temple dedicated to the Hindu god Hanuman in Patna, India. It is one of the most popular temples in the city and is visited by thousands of devotees every day. The temple is located on the banks of the Ganges river and is considered to be one of the most sacred sites in the city. The temple is also known for its annual fair, which is held in the month of February and is attended by people from all over the country.

10. Khuda Baksh Oriental Library

Khuda Baksh Oriental Library is one of the oldest and most prestigious libraries in India. It is located in Patna, the capital of Bihar state. The library was founded in 1891 by Khan Bahadur Khuda Baksh, a wealthy Muslim merchant from Hyderabad. It houses more than 2,00,000 manuscripts, rare books and documents in Persian, Arabic, Urdu, Turkish, Pashto and other languages of the Muslim world. The library also has a rich collection of coins, stamps, paintings, maps and other historical artifacts.
