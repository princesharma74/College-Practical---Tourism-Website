City of historical and cultural importance!

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city has a rich history, and has been a centre of learning and culture since the days of the Maurya and Gupta empires.

Today, Patna is a bustling metropolis, and is home to a number of educational institutions, as well as historical and cultural sites. The city is also a major economic hub, and is a great place to do business.

If you're looking for a city with a rich history and culture, and a bright future, then come to Patna!

Patna Planetarium

Patna Planetarium is a state-of-the-art planetarium located in the heart of the city of Patna, the capital of the Indian state of Bihar. It is one of the largest planetariums in Asia and the only one in India with a Digistar 5 system, the latest in planetarium technology. The planetarium was inaugurated by the then Chief Minister of Bihar, Nitish Kumar, on August 15, 2013.

Spread over an area of two acres, the planetarium has a seating capacity of 250 people. It has a state-of-the-art Digistar 5 system, which is capable of projecting realistic images of the night sky as well as of planets and other astronomical phenomena. The planetarium also has a DLP Cinema system, which can be used to screen educational films on various topics related to astronomy and space science.

The planetarium offers a variety of educational programmes and shows for school and college students, as well as for the general public. It also has a facility for conducting sky-watching programmes, using its powerful rooftop telescope. The planetarium has been developed as a centre for popularising astronomy and space science among the people of Bihar.
