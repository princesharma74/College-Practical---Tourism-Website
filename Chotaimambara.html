City of historical and cultural importance!

Patna, the capital of Bihar, is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. The city has a rich history, and has been a centre of learning and culture since the days of the Maurya and Gupta empires.

Today, Patna is a bustling metropolis, and is home to a number of educational institutions, as well as historical and cultural sites. The city is also a major economic hub, and is a great place to do business.

If you're looking for a city with a rich history and culture, and a bright future, then come to Patna!

Chota Imambara

Chota Imambara, Patna is a beautiful monument located in the heart of the city. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1632. The monument is made of red sandstone and is adorned with intricate carvings and designs. The main entrance of the Imambara is through a majestic archway which leads to a stunning courtyard. The Imambara is also known for its stunning two minarets which were built using jasper and marble.

The interiors of the Imambara are just as stunning as the exterior. The main hall of the Imambara is adorned with delicate plasterwork and is supported by intricately carved pillars. The ceiling of the hall is decorated with beautiful chandeliers and mirror work. The Imambara also has a richly decorated Assembly Hall which was used by the Mughal Emperor for public gatherings.
